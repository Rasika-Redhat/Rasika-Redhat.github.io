Unboxing your Sony Xperia smartphone

Turn the box around and check the specifications of your smartphone (at the bottom of the box). Some of the specifications are given below.

  • Front camera resolution
  • Rear Camera resolution
  • HDR range
  • Internal and extendable storage
  • Processor
  • RAM
  • Battery Capacity
  • USB type


    • Back of Sony Xperia box

Procedure: How to set up your smartphone

  1. Open the box and properly examine your handset. Also, check if the user guide, charger (charging cable as well as the socket) and headphones are present.
  2. Insert the sim cards in slot 1 and slot 2 and micro SD card in the card slot.

    Note: Make sure to insert 4G sim card in slot 1 and 2G sim card in slot 2 as slot 2 does not support 4G sim card.

  3. Switch on the phone and check the battery charge percentage at the top right of your screen.
  4. Select a language.
  5. Set the date and time.
  6. Connect to a Wi-Fi network. Authenticate with Service Set Identifier (SSID) and password.
  7. Login to your Gmail account.

    Note: Make sure to log in with your Gmail account so that you can get a backup of your apps and contacts which are stored on the google drive.

  8. Accept the terms and conditions by tapping on Accept.
  9. Secure your device by tapping on Settings > Lock screen & security. If you lose your device, this security measure prevents unauthorized access to your device. You can set up security in the following ways:
    • face recognition
    • fingerprint recognition
    • simple PIN
    • Pattern
    • Smart Lock
  10. To enable Developer options, tap on Settings > System> About Phone > Build number seven times. In Developer options you can change the default system settings and configuration.

    WARNING: Make sure to make any changes in the Developer options only if you have knowledge about the Android operating system.

  11. Download and update the latest Android version on your smartphone by tapping Settings > About device, then tap System Updates > Check for Updates > Update.

    Note: As you are turning on your Android smartphone for the first time, it might not be running the latest version of the software available. If your smartphone has the latest Android version skip this step.

  12. Call someone and check the sound quality of the microphone as well as the speaker.

You can do much more with your smartphone. Only the basic functions are given in Procedure: How to set up your smartphone. This will help you to get started with your smartphone.
